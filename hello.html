If you've ever been to Cusco, you may have seen Ausangate from a distance. Depending on the season, you may have noticed dark patches peeking through the clouds and wondered: What color is Ausangate? Or, you may be wondering what lies at the mountain's base. If you want to see the mountain more clearly, the best lookout from the city is at San Cristobal. If you want to see the mountain even closer, you may be ready for a trek to meet Ausangate yourself! We spoke with Noah, an adventure photographer from Peru, who shared his knowledge about the remarkable mountain.



Key takaways
  • • Ausangate is a black mountain blanketed with glacial ice and snow.
  • • You can climb Ausangate with the proper training and acclimation to the altitude.
  • • Brilliant blue glacial lakes rest at the base of the mountain. .
  • • The mountain changes from season to season.
  • • To the local people, Ausangate is a living provider.
